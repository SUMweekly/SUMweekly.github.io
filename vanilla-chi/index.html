My name is Vanilla Chi, a New York-based illustrator, amateur graphic designer and animator, part-time art bookmaker. I’m currently a senior in the BFA illustration department at the School of Visual Arts. 

My inspiration comes from my strong nostalgia and personal pain. Because of Covid-19, I have not been back to China for almost two years. I miss my family and hometown. In Chinese traditional culture, we trust everyone has a “root”. For me, the root is like an elastic rope – the longer I am away from home, the more elastic the rope becomes. Now, I almost cannot take the weight of the elasticity, and my work has become a reflection.

Besides, nostalgia can selectively filter memories to beautify or even strengthen the content of yesteryear’s life; it can also expose scars to affirm the present-day me. My artistic expression is actually based on my very personal memories: I always drew girls and dresses when I grew up, because I couldn’t get the dress I liked as a child; I drew bullying and loneliness, because I was hated and disliked by my classmates; I drew carnival and stage show with excessive abundance and bright colors, because I was nostalgic for toys, amusement parks, and carnivals.

I drew expressionless, uniform little boys and girls, because of the pain and depression I feel, but cannot show in concern of annoying people. These experiences constitute who I am I work with my memories in order to produce an honest expression.
