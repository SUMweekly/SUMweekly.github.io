    Dustin Lee is a street photographer currently based in Seoul, South Korea. He takes atmospheric shots of the life he encounters throughout the city, constructing a colourful, dream-like perspective of the scenes that unfold around him. His shots inspire wanderlust, showcasing the excitement that comes from exploration of the city, where one gains brief insights into the characters that navigate through it, as they interact with and become a part of their environment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Dustin has recently published a book entitled Color In Korea<\/a>, containing a series of images taken over his past 6 years living there. Below is an interview from this new book:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Q: What kind of camera do you use?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    I use Leica M3 and M4 with both 35mm and 50mm lenses. 135 format is way to go for me because it is very versatile and quick to use.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Q: How often do you shoot?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Well, I always have my camera with me where ever I go. I shoot pretty often and it’s just part of my daily life routine I should say. But if I do take my time to shoot, I usually photograph at least once every weekend. I try to get one roll per week.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
