We sat down with Tuka, a young London-born singer fresh off the release of her new EP \u2018Folding\u2019. Born to Iraqi parents with a musical background, Tuka spoke about struggling to find similar faces in the music industry and starting out as a singer whilst navigating a pandemic\u2026<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\n
\n

My mum used to be a singer. Not a western pop but the Muslim equivalent of gospel music, she wanted me to go down that path but I was more into Rhianna\u2026.Shakira\u2026.this was at like six years-old. So yeah I\u2019d just watch them and be like that\u2019s what I wanted to do. I wanted to be on stage and sing and make people smile and jump around, the desire to be a singer came from when I was young.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I come from a family with very strong Muslim beliefs so they\u2019re not toooo keen on the route I\u2019m on, even when I\u2019m studying it. I used to study art at college and to them I needed to be someone else, \u2018You need to do Architecture at least? Be a lawyer, a pharmacist, a doctor?\u2019. They\u2019d always try and incorporate art into a more \u2018professional\u2019 setting\u2026 I guess they had higher standards. But they\u2019re letting me do my thing and I\u2019m enjoying it. When I was younger I\u2019d dream about it etcetera etcetera, and eventually that died out after I decided that it wasn\u2019t realistic enough.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
