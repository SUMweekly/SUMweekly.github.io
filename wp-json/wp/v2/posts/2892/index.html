Like most Londoners, Gary the pigeon held a permanent expression of being mildly pissed off. To maintain his persona of relentless irritability, there was an abundance of things to be pissed off about. The weather, Southern Rail, everything being expensive, immigrants, Southern Rail – the list continues. It was with this frame of mind that Gary reluctantly spread his wings, welcoming a new day already dry with disappointment. It was 7:23 on Wednesday morning and the air was soggy and smelt of petrol. Gary could hear the C2 bus rumble across the road, London remerging from what was probably an eventful night yet not in Gary\u2019s memory. His night had involved sitting on top of a lamppost for five hours straight. It was a stale one-dimensional shade of grey.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
