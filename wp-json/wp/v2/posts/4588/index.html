Born in Italy to Ghanaian parents, sound engineer Grace Esia is in her words a \u201cUnicorn\u201d. From dreaming of Beyonce to single handedly running the tech at the recent Bottle Factory event, Grace is force of nature who lights up whatever room she\u2019s in. We sat down with her for a coffee to decompress after a hectic month, and find out how she became one of South-London’s most talented exports.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\n
\n

So what brought you to London?<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

I spent the first 15 years of my life in Italy. I moved to London around 8 years ago and have been here ever since. It was my parents decision really, there wasn\u2019t a discussion it was \u201cYour coming\u201d. Essentially the economy was in a bad way in Italy, lack of Jobs and bad business\u2026<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I\u2019ve been to Italy a few times and obviously you hear stories..how was it growing up as a person of colour there?<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

I feel like I grew up quite naively, my main concern back then was to be the popular girl that everybody liked, I didn\u2019t see those nuances of me being a black person. I just had the regular issues that any other 12-year-old girl would have at that time, I didn\u2019t  really have a first hand experience of racism.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Coming to London was a big shock. I grew up being one of the only black people in my class and even the entire school, to being just anybody in  South-East, one of many!<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
