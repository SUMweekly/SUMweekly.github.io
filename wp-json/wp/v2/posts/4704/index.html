    Temporary Before Permanent is a contemporary art exhibition showcasing prints that were collected and archived over the artists\u2019 time here in the UK. Born and raised in Taiwan, Ann Chang has a background in fine arts, special effects and fashion design. Their artwork showcased their talent brought from all of their past experiences as well as their Taiwanese cultural heritage. With a scent of melancholia, their artwork focuses on bringing joy and laughters to the stressed, anxious modern world.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Originally from a small town based in Taiwan, Ann takes inspiration from the origins of her parents\u2019 home where rain and wet weather are common phenomena. Through connections from back home, as well as friendships built within East London, interactions of culture form many aspects of the artist\u2019s work which are delicately showcased within the amalgamation of this exhibition.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ann beautifully depicts her feelings of nostalgia in the form of delicate carbon prints frozen in time inside a solid resin casing. Although slightly faded, just like her memories of childhood, the sentiment of her rainy hometown is never completely forgotten as those memories are etched into every new drawing of a vase for someone else to keep forever. The delicate carbon prints are contrasted with a solid resin casing which symbolises her desire to preserve her most guarded treasure; her sense of self and belonging. The viewer is invited to open their mind and draw their own conclusions based on this juxtaposition of strongly dissimilar elements.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The first thing you notice in Ann\u2019s drawing is the abundant use of natural elements such as flowers and storms. As you are drawn more into the detail of the designs, the vases begin to tell you a story. For example, on one vase, a young man can be seen crying a tear drop in the shape of a star while his head is stuck in a raging storm, above his head grows a single rose reaching for the sky. You can then export your vision to a fanciful world where the image comes to life as the young man overcomes his sadness, shatters the vase that imprisoned him and blossoms into the person he was born to be.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The artist has an obsession with vases which took her a long time to figure out what it meant to her. She explained \u201cVases are like people, they are in all shapes and colors and it\u2019s up to you what you\u2019d like to put inside of it. The vases I draw are often covered in rain texture which represents the melancholic rainy cities and small towns I live and lived in. \u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    As well as representing a collection of experiences found through human connection, Ann\u2019s visual arts creations aim to present a discovery of peace within all aspects of art-making. Seen as a form of therapeutic experience, there is power in the artwork not only for the artist, but also the people who connect and participate with it, as well as the clients who get to keep it forever.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Framed as a non-aggressive approach to active creation, these prints represent the idea of continuously being able to find joy in a time of challenges and hardships.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
