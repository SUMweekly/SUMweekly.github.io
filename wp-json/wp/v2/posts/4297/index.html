    A compulsion towards the carnivalesque, a vibrant trashy aesthetic and the desire to playfully rub the fantastical against the everyday drive my practice. Through appropriating visual tropes, throwaway narratives and contemporary cultural references and applying a surreal filter to them I create dimly recognizable but imagined worlds. Camp and theatrics are employed to demonstrate the ridiculousness of patriarchal systems and gossip fuels visual flights of fancy as the props of daily life are put on a pedestal.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The tropes of boot, vagina dentata, clitoris eyeball and seeing hands are beginning to form a symbolic language that unifies my practice. Boots, for me, are richly symbolic: Nancy Sinatra’s These Boot Are Made For Walking, Kinky Boots, the saying tough as old boots, the urban dictionaries definition of old boot to describe an old used up vagina, there was an old lady who lived in a shoe, Old Mother Goose and goose stepping. The boot feels like the archetypal solid and well used vessel. In these painted fictions boots grow lips and teeth, fused with the imagery of the toothed vagina, rich in narrative and psychoanalytical symbolism. These creatures emerged out of the first National lockdown and are determined to explore a sense of claustrophobia, crossed boundaries and surreal domestic anticipation.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    I made the installation Join us specifically for the Slade School of Art’s rounded Rotunda, it sees my vagina bootie characters singing a celebratory and inviting song. The installation plays with scale and the merging of sculptural and painterly language. I have been exploring different spiritual spaces and the effect that frescos and statues have on the viewer and poured these experiences into the work. The fantastical world is both playful, yet slightly unnerving: the multiple sculpted oversized figures could be seen as forming a mob.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Here these vagina dentata booties with clitoris eyeballs are not purely defined by their castrating potential: the toothy quims are the protagonists of this universe. They have a collective voice through song: which I composed from their perspective. Receiving The Sarabande Bursary allowed me to work with Lips Choir: A trans inclusive womxn’s choir. This enabled diverse female voices to come together in a celebration that sidesteps patriarchal hero narratives.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The thrust of my work is increasingly focused on exploring space: simultaneously via the performative potential of the picture plane; through treating sculptural objects as flat surface by painting them; and in the creation truly immersive sculptural painted environments. Compositions of frescoes, cult manifestoes, nursery rhymes and the generous grotesque come together under the roofs of my chapel-like dens. These different material languages, signs and concerns are crucially held together by the transformative medium of paint.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
