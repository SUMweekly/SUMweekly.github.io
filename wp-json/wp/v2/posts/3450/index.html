    KIP is a visual arts book about dreaming. Eight creatives with a variety of experience in different fields came together to produce a visual dream diary, offering graphic and photographic representations of what they experience when they’re asleep. The concept came about from a conversation about the links between mental health and sleep; often, people experiencing certain mental health problems can use sleep and dreams as a form of escapism. KIP is an attempt to recreate the hazy, abstract dreamed reality back in the real world.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Curated and designed by Ben Ellis-Rees, KIP features work from Daniela K Monteiro, Milo Blake, Rod Bean & Micky Clutton, Sam Leung, Asel Tambay, Ben Ellis-Rees and Joe Page-Brown. All profits raised are donated directly to the mental health charity Mind. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
