If you\u2019re from a city like London, you can forget that not every part of the UK and Europe is a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities. School trips outside of the capital can be stark reminders that in many parts of the western world, the presence of a person of colour can still garner curiosity and on some occasions genuine hostility.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Originating from Edinburgh, Kimberley Hoyle moved to London in 2016 to pursue a career in the performing arts, experiencing a massive change in her local demographics as a result (8.3% of the Edinburgh population are non-white, compared to 40.2% of London\u2019s population). We sat down with Kimberley to hear about how her experiences differed from that of a Londoner, and how the recent BLM movement allowed her to be better equipped to look back at her childhood with a more critical and informed gaze.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I wouldn\u2019t really put myself down as being just a \u201cdancer\u201d, but that\u2019s what I grew doing and have most experience in at the moment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
