Hope is a PhD student studying the Neuroscience behind the perception of music and sound. When not looking at people\u2019s brains (I forgot to ask if she\u2019s ever touched one and what they smell like, sorry everyone), Hope also is the Founder and Director of Thinking Black,  a social enterprise aiming to empower and amplify young black voices through education and mentorship. In a wide-ranging discussion, Hope touched upon how money can affect people\u2019s perception of what is \u201cHuman\u201d and how the effects of discrimination can effect everything from healthcare to Artificial intelligence.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Tell me about yourself, how did you get to where you are now?<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\n
\n

I\u2019m from Newham in East London and went to a school that used to be pretty bad\u2026.but then got turned around by a \u201cSuper-Headteacher\u201d. At 6th<\/sup>-form I got pulled aside one day and was told that I should apply for Biology at Oxford University, but I knew that my parents wouldn\u2019t let me study anything but Medicine, Engineering or Law. So my teacher had to pretty much convince my mum that going to Oxford to study Biology was actually pretty good ! After a bit I then switched to neuroscience.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Once I graduated I went to New York  where I was a junior research associate in a lab. It was a really cool experience, I helped PhD students set up and run their experiments and had a team of undergrads working under me too which was fun.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
