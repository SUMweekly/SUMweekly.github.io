    I learnt to tattoo at a shop in West Norwood called Studiotrap when I was still at school. Just cleaning the shop and drawing, After a few months I was sometimes allowed to practice on myself or a friend. Once school ended I worked there full time, and stayed for a couple of years before moving to Inkhouse in Brixton. There my style developed into what I’m more known for now, which is single needle black and grey. Post-pandemic I’ll be working at Rye Lanez in Peckham.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    When I started working in Brixton, people mostly wanted chicano, gangster style black and grey realism. This mainly involved a lot of printing images of girls or lions or whatever straight off Google. Fair enough if that’s what you’re into, but at that time I was trying to do colourful traditional tattoos. No one wanted those, so when I was drawing flash I started trying to mix the timeless designs of that bold, traditional and most importantly hand-drawn tattooing with the kind of fine line, black and grey tattoos people were after. People seemed to like that stuff. I’ve done a lot of single needle dragons now.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    I do a lot of drawing outside tattooing too. I’m currently working on some illustrations for websites, business cards, etc., and have also done a few short, image-only stories of my own. In climate crisis times, I feel like we need to appreciate our surroundings more, and in my stories I try to draw attention to whatever weird narratives we might find in everyday life. Pre-covid I was attending the London Atelier of Representational Art (LARA), an amazing place which massively improved my drawing. Sadly the school closed permanently after lockdown, leading me to look elsewhere to continue my studies. I’m now in my first year of BA Drawing down at Falmouth University, but I’m still taking commissions and tatting back in London in the school holidays. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
