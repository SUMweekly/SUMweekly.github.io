    Coming from Austria via Belgium, Fiona is a mixed-heritage artist who confronts issues of race, belonging and identity in her work. She spoke to SUM about her experiences in the commercial design industry and how she uses her art to understand and reflect the many cultures she has experienced in her path as an artist.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
    \n

    Fiona: I\u2019d call myself a multimedia artist\u2026which is kind of a basic explanation, but maybe with a focus on design? My work revolves around belonging, heritage and race. I\u2019m trying to underline the marginalised voices of people who don\u2019t have enough say in our society.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    I grew up in Brussels, my mums Austrian and my Dads Nigerian and I was raised the \u201cAustrian Way\u201d. I was never perceived as a Belgian, nor as an Austrian or Nigerian person. I always wanted to study philosophy, but I liked the artists approach to viewing the world and enjoyed experimenting with colour etc\u2026 so I thought \u201cfuck it\u201d let\u2019s be an artist. I\u2019m now doing a course on advertising and brand design; I get to explore quite a wide range of different mediums, but the focus is very commercially driven and whitewashed. The way the course educates us to fit into the white industry isn\u2019t enjoyable, but at the same time, it\u2019s helped me think critically and given me a wider experience.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
