    Christopher Samuel Idowu is a Nigerian visual artist from Badagry, Lagos who hails from the Yoruba and Ogu speaking clan. His elder brother’s doodles in comic books served as an inspiration for art that began in his childhood.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Following his passion for drawing, since 2015 he has studied at Yaba College of Technology where he majored in painting. Outside his studies he has also interned with Space29.7 under the mentorship of renowned Nigerian painter, Raji Mohammed B. Spirituality including Christian iconography and belief system are influences to his painting and drawing practice.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Christopher explores and documents earthly and ethereal moments using studies of the human form taken from his immediate environment; fusing abstraction with portraiture were necessary for different forms through drawings and paintings. His renditions have a trace to symbolism. He works in different media including Conte crayon, charcoal, watercolours, acrylics and oils to convey a visual commentary on societal contradictions through distinctly selected subjects. During his working process, at some point he allows his inspirations and ideas to choose their suitable materials and techniques to work with. He perceives himself to be a tree with branches at different stages of growth, but the same fruit.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    His ideology and philosophy view art as an integral part of human existence; in this view, he considers himself experiential. He has participated in several group exhibitions in Nigeria including the ICAF (International Culture and Art Festival) Art show in Lagos in, 2018 and RELE boot camp art exhibition, Ado- Ekiti, in 2019, as part of the residency programme for emerging artists.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Asa Cohort of Art in Medicine 2018, he worked with other artists to create a mural at the bone marrow transplant ward of Lagos State University Teaching Hospital (LUTH). He is a recent recipient of the TILGA fund grant collaboration with RELE gallery, in 2020. His grant was awarded for a project titled “CONDOLENCE BOOK” which he explored the themes of mortality, love, grief, healing and the afterlife using the canvas as an adopted condolence book.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    In 2020, he was an artist- in- residence at the nomad artist residency for three days at Shores Hotels, Lagos. This residency is an initiative of the artist, Adewale Alimi, bringing together artists to respond to the theme of “Studio in the head”.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
