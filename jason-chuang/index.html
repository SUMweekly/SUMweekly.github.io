Jason Chuang is an illustrator and storyteller from Taiwan, based in the UK. His practice focuses on the exploration of human emotions, through the creation of symbolic imageries coated with elements of the absurd and poetries. 

Jason: I am naturally attracted to the beauty and oddities that occur in everyday life and these interests are translated into the work that I produce. I am hugely inspired by the surrealist movement, as it resonates with the way I work and my visuals are heavily reliant on the unconscious mind and intuitions when reflecting on my life experiences.

My passion lies in narratives, and I often spend my spare time pondering on impossible situations, projecting myself into endless alternate universes, constantly in a search of new ways to tell stories.

Under the impact of Covid-19, I have spent an unusually vast amount of time indoors, which I have used to self-reflect and explore ways to convey my experiences through various visual metaphors.

The above series of works were created during the self-isolation period when every day seemed to merge into an endless cycle and becoming harder and harder to differentiate. Emancipating ideas and directing my emotions out from my body, is both a meditative and healing condolence.

To keep up with Jason’s work, you can follow him on Instagram. You can also check his shop, which sells prints here.
