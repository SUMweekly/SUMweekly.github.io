Art / Russell Herron

May 2, 2021May 6, 2021 Art Russell Herron

Russell Herron is a London based artist who has worked across various media, but over the last few years has become known for a series of pencil drawings of cardboard faces. During the lockdowns of 2020 he drew 100 of these small cardboard faces, selling them across the world through his Instagram feed. ‘It was like these lost souls were finding homes’, he says. ‘People really seemed to connect with them.’ 

Each one begins as a face made from cardboard, which Herron then reproduces in meticulous detail as a pencil drawing. ‘They are like portraits, but without an identity’, he says. ‘They are all influenced by the soup of images I carry round in my head; from childhood cartoon characters, film stars, artworks, medical textbooks, photographers, album covers, it all comes out somehow.’ 

These drawings reflect his on-going interest in the broad themes of identity, history and place, concerns that have informed all his work. 

He published a book of the one hundred portraits, which, although initially just meant for collectors, quickly sold out. A second edition followed and this too was snapped up. In 2021 he is continuing to draw the small portraits, and continuing to find them new homes, but also making time to work on larger pieces. ‘More portraits of lost souls,’ he says, ‘just bigger.’
